A hundred years won’t put a mark on paper,
It wouldn’t now, so why then?
Do we fear the dust will haunt the margins -
Cut, weave, and erase its way one day to the next,
Leaving the faint notes of a pen,
Once held with great respect.

Through cold rain beats the path,
The shimmer of a stone will meet your eye,
As it shot its arc and to the ground,
You are throwing the sun around the earth:
Bound to its rhythms, it relies on you to see,
Every star which hides behind a jagged countertop must rise to complement those free.
